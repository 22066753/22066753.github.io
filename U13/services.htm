EE stands for Everything Everywhere Limited is a British national mobile network operator and internet service provider, which is a brand within the BT Group. The company was formed as Everything Everywhere in 2010

EE runs the UK's biggest and fastest mobile network, pioneering the UK's first superfast 4G mobile service in October 2012. EE's 4G coverage today reaches 90% of the UK geography and 99% of the population.

EE, part of the BT Group, runs the UK's biggest and fastest mobile network, offering 4G in more places than any other UK network. EE was first to launch 4G in the UK in October 2012, and also first to launch 5G in May 2019.


Telefonica UK Limited is a British telecommunications services provider, headquartered in Slough, England which operates under the O2 brand (styled as O₂ ). The company was formed on 7 January 1985 as Cellnet, a joint venture between BT Group and Securicor. Cellnet was one of the first two mobile networks in the UK, alongside Vodafone.

O2 publicly announced on 15 December 2009 that it had successfully demonstrated a 4G connection using LTE technology installed in six masts in Slough.[40] The technology, which was supplied by Huawei, achieved a peak downlink rate of 150 Mbps.

O₂ plc was purchased by the Spanish telecommunications company Telefónica in 2006 for £18 billion. O2 and Vodafone signed a deal in June 2012 which saw the two companies 'pool' their network technology, creating a single national grid of 18,500 transmitter sites. They will remain as two separate companies though.

O2 currently sponsor the England rugby team, Arsenal Football Club, the Channel 4 reality TV show Big Brother, and a load of music venues and festivals across the country. In 2007 the Millennium Dome changed its name to the O2 and is now a big entertainment venue.


Three is the owner of several mobile phone networks and broadband Internet providers, which operate in Hong Kong, Macau, Austria, Denmark, Indonesia, Ireland, Italy, Sweden, and the United Kingdom. The brand was officially started on March 3, 2003 in Hong Kong. In 2022, There were over 110 million Three customers worldwide.

The logo, which is a visual representation of the number "3", was originally three-dimensional and orange in colour. Three was launched as the UK's first commercial video mobile network on 3 March 2003 (03-03-03), the day that 3G services went live across the country.

Three has announced a £2 billion investment plan to upgrade its mobile network, and plans to improve 4G network speeds and roll out more 5G services . The plans say that Three wants to improve speeds by up to 150%. Lots of other operators use the Three UK network, including: SMARTY, iD Mobile, and FreedomPop. Three has 99% population coverage on 3G and 4G networks. They have a similar level of coverage to the other main mobile networks.


The name Vodafone comes from voice data fone. It operates services in Asia, Africa, Europe, and Oceania. As of January 2023, Vodafone owns and operates networks in 21 countries. Vodafone was launched on 1 January 1985, with its first office based in the Courtyard in Newbury, Berkshire, UK.

Vodafone sponsored Manchester United from 2000 until the 2005–06 season. In 2007, Vodafone entered into a sponsorship deal with the McLaren Formula One team (previously Vodafone sponsored Ferrari in 2002 until 2006), which traded as "Vodafone McLaren Mercedes" until the sponsorship ended at the end of the 2013 season.

In September 2010, an investigation revealed certain details of Vodafone's tax avoidance activities. It was reported that Vodafone set up companies to avoid paying tax. The news of this legal tax avoidance sparked angry protests, beginning in October 2010, outside Vodafone shops across the UK. This meant lots of their shops shut. Vodafone was heavily criticised due to poor customer service and severe technical inadequacies, which earned them their nickname "Vodafail" – a website of the same name still exists. In 2019, Vodafone was rated as the UK's worst mobile network provider for the eighth year in a row, in the eighth survey by Which? magazine.
